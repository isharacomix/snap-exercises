A program in SNAP is made up of Blocks. To write a program, you drag blocks from the Corral on the left into the Script Window to the right. As you can see, the blocks look like puzzle pieces. If you line up the puzzle pieces, the two blocks will snap together, creating a Script.

If you want to know what a block or a script does, just quickly click the block or script one time. To complete this lab:

  • Drag the two blocks to the script area. Click on them to see what happens.
  • Snap the blocks together. Click on the script to see what happens.
  • Click on "show answer" and create the script displayed there.
  • After seeing the answer, click on the green "Next" button in the top right to move on to the next part of the lab.
